Frequently Asked Questions

  • What does Guage mean on Liners?
    A term to describe thickness. LDPE and LLDPE can liners are measured by mil thickness and HMW-HDPE can liners are measured by micron thickness.
  • What does Micron mean for liners?
    Term used in the measurement of HMW-HD can liners. 25.4 microns = .001". 1000 microns (M) = 1mm. HMW-HDPE can liners are 6 to 24 microns.
  • What does Mil mean on liners?
    Term used in the measurement of LDPE and LLDPE can liners. 1 mil = .001". Can liners between .35 to 4.0 mil.
  • What are the types of resin used in making liners?
    The three types of PE resins are LDPE, LLDPE and HMW-HDPE.
  • What is HDPE (High Density Polyethylene)?
    Bags made from HDPE resin provide excellent film strength and puncture resistance, but less tear resistance than LLDPE.
  • What is LLDPE (Linear Low Density Polyethylene)?
    This is the primary type of resin used in modern can liner manufacturing technology. Bags made from LLDPE resin provide an excellent combination of film strength, puncture resistance and tear resistance.
  • What are Reprocessed Bags made from?
    Reprocessed Bags refers to resin that has been used at least once before. Can be post-industrial (scrap) or post-consumer (recycling). The property of the resin is decreased each time it is reused.
  • Why are liners made from Metallocene resins better?
    Metallocene is the latest resin technology producing the strongest bags ever made. It has all the strength of high density bags without the zippering. It has up to 10 times the dart strength of other bags, along with tear strength and elasticity of hexene liners. It meets performance specifications for both high density and linear low liners.
  • Why is the HexSeal a better seal, and how is the HexSeal different from a star seal?
    Gusseted bags and flat bottom bags do not evenly distribute weight and are much more likely to leak, while HexSeal bags are 99.9% leakproof. Because of the unique construction, bags with a HexSeal will hold more volume than bags with a star seal, and do not need extra length to compensate.
  • What are the types of seals on liners?
    Seal is a term used to describe the bottom of a can liner. The three types of seals are flat, gusseted, and star.
  • What is a Star Seal liner?
    This multi-layered seal is full gusseted and then folded prior to sealing, allowing for the trash to be evenly distributed.
  • What is a Gusset Seal liner?
    A flat-style bag manufactured with both sides tucked in to form gussets. Has a tendency to leak wet trash from the center at gusset points where four layers of film meet two.
  • What is a Flat Seal liner?
    A straight seal along the bottom of a can liner (looks like a pillow case). Though Flat Seals are strong, they may have a tendency to leak wet trash from the corners.
  • What are Cored Rolls liners?
    Can liners are rolled together on cardboard cylinders (looks similar to a roll of paper towels). Can liners come inside a special box that dispenses them with ease.
  • What is a Top-Side Dispenser Box?
    An innovative style of box that allows stacking in small spaces. It is just as easy to pull a can liner from the side as it is from the top.
  • What are Individually Folded liners?
    Can liners are separately folded, then stacked on top of one another. This allows the end-user to pull liners out of the box with much more ease vs. bulk-folded bags.
  • What is Wet Load Capacity?
    Measurement of how much wet weight a can liner will hold.
  • What is Dry Load Capacity?
    Measurement of how much dry weight a can liner will hold.
